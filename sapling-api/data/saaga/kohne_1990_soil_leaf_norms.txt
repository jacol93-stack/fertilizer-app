============================================================================
INTRODUCTION
============================================================================

Three working-group conclusions:
  1. Standardise the index leaf used for leaf analysis.
  2. Re-establish leaf-analysis norms via research using that standardised leaf.
  3. Adopt the phenological growth-cycle approach for avocado tree management.

============================================================================
SITE SELECTION (citing Nel 1983)
============================================================================

Per Nel (1983), an avocado-suitable soil should have:
  - Red colour
  - Clay content 20-40%
  - Well-drained
  - No compacted, clayey or patchy layers
  - Soil depth > 2 m
  - pH (water) ~ 6.0

============================================================================
ESTABLISHMENT (Years 1-3)
============================================================================

Bower (1980): no extra fertiliser in the planting hole.
Koen & Langenegger (1980): year-1 trees do well with no soil-applied fertiliser.

Foliar Zn + B essential because suitable soils are typically very low in both
(Wolstenholme 1987). Apply monthly during active flushing:
  - Zinc: 200 g zinc oxide / 100 L water
  - Boron: 100 g Solubor / 100 L water
  - Combinable in one tank; spray during cool periods.

Chronic boron deficiencies: soil Solubor 4 g/m² drip area, spring or summer
(Whiley pers comm).

============================================================================
LEAF AND SOIL ANALYSIS
============================================================================

Sampling
--------
Leaf samples: from non-fruiting branches not showing flush signs (Koen &
Langenegger 1981). Take when the summer flush ends (March/April). Same trees
sampled season to season.

Soil samples: from a uniform sampling area; two depths (0-15 cm and 15-30 cm)
kept separate; 500 g - 1 kg per analysis.

============================================================================
INTERPRETATION
============================================================================

Compare each year's results vs Table 2 norms and previous results. Above-
normal results: cut out the relevant fertiliser entirely for a year+. "It is
worse to overreact and apply fertiliser, than to do nothing if any of the
factors affecting results are in question" (Partridge 1989).

============================================================================
FERTILISATION IN ESTABLISHED ORCHARDS
============================================================================

Time per phenological cycle (Fig. 1, Fig. 2). Three growth stages: root,
shoot, reproductive.

Manipulator vs non-manipulator nutrients:
  - Nitrogen: ONLY manipulator. Timing critical; apply Jan/Feb after fruit-drop.
    Earlier = vegetative growth → increased fruit-drop. Summer-flush nutrition
    influences current + future productivity (Whiley, Saranah, Cull, Pegg 1988).
  - Non-manipulators: P, K, Zn, B, Ca, Mg, S, Fe, Cu, Mn. Broad optimal band;
    timing not critical but maintain levels in soil + leaf optimal ranges.

============================================================================
MULCHING
============================================================================

Cover crops: legumes where soil low N + root rot present. High C:N covers
(oats, maize, wheat, napier grass) where soil N excessive. Oats may also
antagonise Phytophthora (Wolstenholme 1990).
