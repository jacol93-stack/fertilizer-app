## Avocado (Persea americana)

Cultivar coverage below: Hass (default for SA), Fuerte (Du Plessis & Koen 1992 trial cv.), Pinkerton, Reed, Lamb Hass — last three have no cultivar-specific N norm in SA literature; Köhne 1990 lumps them as "Other".

### Growth stages (SA, Limpopo / Mpumalanga / KZN dominant)

| Month (SA) | Stage | Key process | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Jun – Sep | Flowering (winter–spring) | Bud differentiation → bloom; cool months Jun–Aug critical (<20 °C upsets set) | [T1 SAAGA — Robertson 1969 via southafrica.co.za] |
| Sep – Dec | Pollination, fruit set, fruit drop | Critical 90-day window; N applied here drives veg growth → fruit drop. AVOID N. | [T1 Köhne 1990 §Fertilisation] |
| Jan – Feb | Post-set / fruit hold; spring flush ends | Apply N here (after fruit drop, before summer flush). | [T1 Köhne 1990; T1 Whiley et al. 1988] |
| Feb – Apr | Fruit fill (early Hass / Fuerte harvest start) | Summer flush; index-leaf sampling Mar–Apr (6–8 mo old) | [T1 Du Plessis & Koen 1992 §Materials and Methods] |
| Mar – Aug | Fuerte harvest (Levubu Feb–Apr; KZN May–Aug) | Cultivar-dependent maturity | [T1 SAAGA via southafrica.co.za] |

| Aug – Oct | Lamb Hass / Reed / late Carmen-Hass | Latest cultivars; Ryan up to Oct in KZN | [T1 SAAGA] |

Alternate bearing: pronounced in Hass under SA/NZ conditions; on-year nutrient demand can be 2× off-year (NZ data — leaf K drops, leaf Ca/Mg/S/Zn rise with yield class) [T2 Dixon 2008 NZAGA].

### Notes

- **Cultivar differences in leaf N**: Hass needs ~2.2 % leaf N for optimal yield; Fuerte ~1.8–2.0 %; "Other" cv (incl. Pinkerton/Reed/Lamb Hass) ~1.9–2.2 %. Excess N in Fuerte actively reduces yield (Du Plessis & Koen 1992 Table 3 — N7 dropped to 15.2 kg/tree from N3 73 kg/tree).
- **Salinity sensitivity**: avocado is one of the most salt-sensitive tree crops; rootstock + leaf-Cl monitoring critical. Soil resistance >500 Ω target [T1 Köhne 1990].
- **Phytophthora root rot**: dominant biotic risk; Ca improves root resistance; oats cover crop antagonises *P. cinnamomi* [T1 Köhne 1990; T2 Crowley 2011].
- **Foliar effectiveness**: thick waxy avocado cuticle limits foliar uptake [T2 Crowley 2011] — except Zn/B which are well-documented to respond.
- **N timing rule** (Köhne 1990): N is the ONLY manipulator nutrient; pre-bloom N → vegetative growth competes with fruit set → fruit drop. Apply N Jan–Feb in SA after fruit drop.
- **N-source pH**: ammonium-bearing fertilisers (urea, AS) lower soil pH; nitrate fertilisers (CN, KNO₃) raise pH and can induce Zn/Fe deficiency [T2 Crowley 2011].

### Nutrient losses (QDAF 2015, Australia — useful for SA fert-budget engine)

- N: 30–50 % lost to leaching + volatilisation

### Notes

- **Cultivar split**: SA Sensation studies (Stassen et al. 1997, 2000): N, Mg, Ca content of fruits decrease with tree age — Ca decrease most pronounced → relevant to soft-nose susceptibility. Sensation removal dominated by Ca + K; Australian Kensington dominated by N + K. Engine should NOT use a single cultivar removal value across cultivars.
- **Soft-nose / internal breakdown**: keep N/Ca <0.5; K/Ca <0.2.
- **DRIS norms** (Hundal 2005, Punjab): N 0.92–1.37, P 0.08–0.16, K 0.21–0.44, Ca 1.71–3.47, Mg 0.15–0.37, S 0.09–0.19 % — narrower than R&R standard for Ca, K.
- **Loss factors**: see QDAF 2015 list above. Engine should apply efficiency factors when converting removal → applied rate (esp. P recovery 20–50 %, B recovery 40 %).
- **Pruning impact**: removed branches/leaves carry significant nutrients — Cruz-Barron 2013 fruit-only vs whole-tree Ataulfo NPK ratios show ~3× multiplier where heavy annual pruning is practiced.
