# Citrus — South Africa data extract (2026-04-27)

Compiled by depth-first extraction from a small set of high-tier sources. Tier tags match the
session source priority. Where a single ranged value is published by SA Tier 1, the band is
surfaced as-is (no point-picking). Where SA-specific data is unavailable, an international Tier 2
parallel is used and explicitly tagged.

**Sample-leaf convention used throughout:** all leaf-norm tables in this file are calibrated to
the international standard of **4–6 month-old spring-flush leaves from non-fruiting terminals**
(UF/IFAS / Haifa / California-DRIS standard). South African and South American practice is to
sample leaves from **fruit-bearing terminals**, which run **lower in N, P, K and higher in Ca, Mg**
than the same-age leaf from a non-fruiting terminal — flagged in the Notes per crop
[T2 Haifa Citrus Guide §2.2.1; T1 Citrus Academy NQ-2 §4.1].

When converting a fruit-bearing-terminal leaf result to the non-fruiting-terminal scale below,
expect roughly +0.1–0.2 % N, +0.01–0.02 % P, +0.1–0.3 % K, −0.3–0.5 % Ca, −0.05 % Mg as a
practical rule of thumb [T2 Haifa Citrus Guide §2.2.1].

Cultivar-specific harvest months are tabulated under each crop. Vegetative + reproductive

### Genus-level soil thresholds (SA practice)
SA convention is **pH(H2O) target 6.5–7.5** for citrus [T1 Citrus Academy NQ-2 §3.1]. When
pH(H2O) < 5.3 aluminium becomes phytotoxic to roots; when pH(H2O) > 7.5, P, Zn, Mn become
unavailable [T1 Citrus Academy NQ-2 §3.1]. The SA "fertility ideal" reads as a base-cation
ratio rather than mg/kg thresholds:

| Soil component | Target | Source |
|---|---|---|
| pH(H2O) | 6.5–7.5 | [T1 Citrus Academy NQ-2 §3.1] |
| pH(KCl) | ≥5.5 (replant requirement to fix sub-soil before establishment) | [T1 SAFJ April-2022 CRI Short-Course p.56] |
| P (Bray-1) | 25–50 mg/kg | [T1 Citrus Academy NQ-2 §3.4] |
| K (% of CEC) | 5–7.5 % | [T1 Citrus Academy NQ-2 §3.4] |

| Mg (% of CEC) | 20–25 % | [T1 Citrus Academy NQ-2 §3.4] |
| Na (% of CEC) | <1 % | [T1 Citrus Academy NQ-2 §3.4] |
| Effective rooting depth | ≥500 mm | [T1 Citrus Academy NQ-2 §2.3] |

higher in Nadorcott — a known feature of late-mandarin biomass partitioning.

### Genus-level per-stage demand split

SA / Florida convention is to divide annual N into **3 roughly-equal increments** for bearing
trees [T2 UF/IFAS SS675 §"Application Timing for Bearing Trees"]:

| Stage | % of annual N | Window (Florida — ~Northern hemisphere) | SA equivalent (mirror calendar) | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-flowering / spring flush | ~33 % | early Feb → flowering | early Aug → flowering | [T2 UF/IFAS SS675] |
| Flowering → fruit-set | ~33 % | flowering → mid-June | flowering → mid-Dec | [T2 UF/IFAS SS675] |
| Fruit fill / sizing | ~33 % | mid-June → mid-Oct (avoid summer rains) | mid-Dec → mid-Apr (avoid summer rains in Lowveld) | [T2 UF/IFAS SS675] |

California refines this to: **"about two-thirds of the annual N requirement should be supplied
between early spring flush and fruit set"** [T2 CDFA-FREP Citrus]. UF/IFAS rule for **K**: apply
K2O at **1.0–1.25× the annual N rate** (raise to 1.25× when leaf-K consistently sub-optimum)
[T2 UF/IFAS cg091 §K]. No cited per-stage % split for K; treat as following the same 3-way
split as N.

### Notes
- Brittle wood is a known cultivar vulnerability in Valencia — agronomists in Letsitele cap N below
  the high end of the Haifa range to limit wind damage [T2 SA Fruit Journal "Valencia orange
  selections Letsitele citrus production region" qualitative].
- Late Valencias (McLean, Du Roi) overlap with the start of the next bloom. Sample leaves
  **before** July picking for late cultivars, between February and May per SA convention
  [T1 Citrus Academy NQ-2 §4.1].
- Sample-bearing-terminal correction: SA labs report leaf-N ~0.1–0.2 % lower than the
  Haifa/UF non-fruiting-terminal table; budget for the offset before flagging deficiency
  [T2 Haifa Citrus Guide §2.2.1].

### Notes
- **Granulation** (cell-wall breakdown / juice loss) is the headline Navel-specific disorder — driven
  by under-supplied Ca/K during cell-division and over-supplied N late-season. Cap N rate at the
  middle of the orange band for late-Navel cultivars [T2 Yara US "Role of Nitrogen in Citrus"].
- Navels are seedless triploids — fruit set is more sensitive to bloom-stage stress than Valencia.
  Avoid N applications during late bloom (mid-Aug → early Sep) on stressed orchards [T2 Yara
  US "Citrus growth stages — Flowering"].
- Cara Cara (red-flesh Navel) shares the same nutritional regime as classic Navel.

## Major gaps (data NOT found in this pass)

1. **Raath 2021 SA citrus handbook (R250 print, no free PDF)** — the canonical SA fertilisation
   reference. Confirmed by SAFJ April-2022 short-course article that this handbook is the
   industry standard but is a paid product. No free portions found online. Purchase at
   https://www.citrusres.com/product/handbook-for-fertilisation-of-citrus-in-south-africa-pieter-raath-2021/
   would close this gap and likely overwrite ~30 % of the Haifa-based numbers above with SA-
   specific calibrations.
2. **CRI Production Guidelines Volume II Chapter 1 — Fertilisation** — referenced by Citrus
   Academy NQ-2 bibliography but no public free PDF found (cri.co.za certificate currently
   broken in this session). The Citrus Resource Warehouse (citrusresourcewarehouse.org.za)
   has the document but its file links return 403 Forbidden without member login.
3. **Sulphur leaf-norm bands** — UF/IFAS lists S in tissue testing but does not publish
   deficient/low/optimum/high quantitative bands for citrus.
4. **Mandarin per-ton micronutrient removal** — Haifa Table 6 only gives macros for "Mandarin";
   the same row is used as a proxy for Nules/Nadorcott/Tango/Clementine (no per-cultivar
   variant published).
5. **Pomelo SA commercial data** — no SA published yield, leaf-norm, or removal data; everything
   is grapefruit-proxied above.
6. **Per-stage demand split for K, P, Ca, Mg** — global literature publishes only the **N split
   (3 × 33 %)**. K/P/Ca/Mg splits are not quantified per stage; Sapling's engine should default
   K to N split, P to a single Aug application, Ca to cell-division window (Oct–Nov SA), Mg as

7. **Rootstock multipliers (quantified)** — the SA literature refers qualitatively to rough lemon /
   Carrizo / X639 / C35 / Swingle differences but no published "× factor" by rootstock × cultivar
   combination. This is a known agronomy gap globally.
8. **Saline-irrigation adjustment factors** — Citrusdal / Lower Orange River growers use saline
   water; no quantified leaf-norm or rate adjustment table. Haifa offers narrative ("switch to
   KNO3 from KCl") but no multiplier.
