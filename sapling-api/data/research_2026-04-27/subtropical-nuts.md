### Per-tree N rate by age (FERTASA/SAMAC convention)
- Recommended: 25–50 g N per tree per year of age, capped at 500 g/tree from year 10 onwards [T1 FERTASA 5.8.1 §N].
- P annual: 7–10 g per tree per year of age (≈ 1/5 of N) [T1 FERTASA 5.8.1 §P].
- K:N ratio 1:1 up to bearing age; 1.25:1 to 1.5:1 from year 5 [T1 FERTASA 5.8.1 §K].

### Per-stage nutrient demand split
FERTASA prescribes timing rules (no explicit %) — codified here:
- **Producing orchards**: N applied **only** Mar–Oct (i.e. 0% Nov–Feb to avoid vegetative growth competing with oil accumulation) [T1 FERTASA 5.8.1 §N]. Implied even split across 8 months.
- **K**: best applied winter → early summer for new growth + nut development [T1 FERTASA 5.8.1 §K].
- **B foliar**: Sep–Dec, up to 4 sprays [T1 FERTASA 5.8.1 §B].
- **Zn**: spring + early summer, but apply when soils are warm enough for uptake [T1 FERTASA 5.8.1 §Zn].

Per-stage % split (codified from above):

|---|---|---|---|---|

### Notes
- Macadamia is **B-sensitive**: foliar excess phytotoxic; >100 mg/kg leaf B = warning. Soil + foliar must be combined since B is immobile in tree [T2 Yara AU §B; T1 FERTASA §B].
- Excess N → wind damage (lanky growth), weak framework, no yield benefit [T1 FERTASA 5.8.1 §N].
- Mulching from inter-row + chopped pruning critical to keep OC ≥1.5%; below that = urgent intervention [T1 FERTASA 5.8.1 §OM].
- Roots sensitive to high topsoil pH: surface lime contraindicated, especially on young trees [T1 FERTASA 5.8.1 §Ca].
- Zn–P antagonism: high P → induced Zn deficiency in spring/early summer (cool soil) [T1 FERTASA 5.8.1 §Zn].
- "Nut-in-shell" definition matters: Yara figures appear to be commercial NIS at ~10% moisture; SAMAC/Schoeman figures are DIS at 3.5% — convert before using.

| Parachute leaf stage | Ni | foliar; product label rates | [T1 Schmidt 2021 §Ni]; [T2 UGA Bulletin 1332] |
| Cu deficiency | Cu | from leaf analysis; Cu often = disease control | [T1 Schmidt 2021 §Cu] |
| Texas Zn programme — alternative | Zn | green-tip + 1, 3, 5–6, 8 weeks after green-tip | [T1 Schmidt 2021 citing Begnaud 2012 TX] |

**Important: don't mix ZnNO₃ with Cu oxychloride** [T1 FERTASA 5.8.2 §Note].

### Adjustments
| Factor | Rule | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Foliar Zn application direction | spray from soil upwards (abaxial absorbs better than adaxial) | [T1 Schmidt 2021 §Zn] |
| Sudden between-season fertilizer change | avoid — worsens alternate bearing amplitude | [T1 Schmidt 2021 §Mgmt] |
| Soil EC > 250 mS/m | yield × 0.9 | [T1 Schmidt 2021] |
| Soil EC > 350 mS/m | yield × 0.75 | [T1 Schmidt 2021] |
| Soil EC > 490 mS/m | yield × 0.5 | [T1 Schmidt 2021] |
| Pre-plant — physical layers | rip out before planting (irreversible if missed) | [T1 Schmidt 2021 §Establishment] |
| First years post-plant if soil pre-remediated | minimal fertilization until yr 4 | [T1 Schmidt 2021 §Establishment] |
| Planting pit — no/minimal N; emphasize P, K, Zn (+ Mg, Ca for roots) | low-salt-index products | [T1 Schmidt 2021 §Establishment] |
| Lime older orchards (FERTASA) | broadcast incorporation rarely successful — pre-plant only | [T1 FERTASA 5.8.2 §Pre-plant] |

### Notes
- Pecan is **mycorrhizal** → poor correlation between soil P and uptake; root volume/distribution unknown [T1 Schmidt 2021 citing Woodroof & Woodroof 1934, Cooke 1982].
- US norms vary widely east→west — geography matters; pick the analogue (Georgia ≈ Mpumalanga subtropical; New Mexico/Arizona ≈ NW/Vaalharts) [T1 Schmidt 2021 §Tbl 2].
- **Salt-sensitive** crop; SA producers in Vaalharts/Orange River region must monitor EC quarterly.
- N:K leaf ratio target 2:1 — actionable management lever [T1 Schmidt 2021 citing Wells & Wood 2007].
- Excess N → husk fails to split open → quality loss [T1 FERTASA 5.8.2 §N].
- Mg deficiency on acid soils <pH 6.5 [T1 FERTASA 5.8.2 §Mg].
- Alternate bearing is **the** dominant production challenge — flower differentiation depends on previous-season reserves; nutritional consistency between seasons matters more than peak rates.

### Crop nutrient requirements per ton
**GAP** — no published per-t removal coefficient in OSU EM-9080 or BC Ref Guide. Hazelnuts are programmed by leaf analysis and tree-age formulae, not removal arithmetic.

### Per-stage nutrient demand split
- N: spring application after active growth begins (matches stored-reserve uptake window) [T2 OSU EM-9080 §N].
- K: timing flexible; muriate of potash before mid-Feb to avoid Cl toxicity, otherwise potassium sulphate [T2 OSU EM-9080 §K].
- B foliar: May (OR) → **Nov in SA** [T2 OSU EM-9080 §B].
- Mg: spring with N application [T2 OSU EM-9080 §Mg].

### Notes
- **Eastern Filbert Blight (EFB)** is now the dominant SA-relevance constraint — cultivar choice (Jefferson, Yamhill = EFB-resistant) matters more than nutrition for new plantings [T2 BC Ref Guide §Background, §Disease].
- Hazelnuts are wind-pollinated → orchard layout (pollinizer placement) is a separate engineering question.
- Storage moisture target ≤10% [T2 BC Ref Guide §Background].
- **No SA-specific nutrition data exists.** This crop should likely not be in the SA recommendation engine v1; flag as "unsupported" until SAMAC-equivalent association exists.
