Citation convention: `[T1 Source — section]`. Tomato + sweet pepper tables (Starke Ayres) are the depth gold standard among SA Tier 1 sources for vegetables.

> **Important note on Starke Ayres "weekly splits"**: the memory note flagged Starke Ayres as the SA source most likely to publish week-by-week schedules. The 2019 production guidelines do publish stage-by-stage splits for tomato, sweet pepper, sweet corn, butternut, onion, cabbage, broccoli and cauliflower — these are reproduced verbatim below. They are typically given as **block windows (Week 0–6, 6–12, 12–18 …)** rather than per-week values; do not invent finer granularity than the source publishes.

Type-cycle reference (transplant → end of harvest):
- Strong determinate: 18 wk total, 4–6 wk harvest [T1 Starke Ayres 2019 Table 4 comments]

- Indeterminate: 25 wk total, ~12 wk harvest [Table 6]

Temperature requirements [T1 Starke Ayres 2019 Table 1]:
- Germination: 16–29 °C optimum (min 11, max 34)
- Vegetative: 21–24 °C
- Fruit set night: 14–17 °C; day 19–24 °C

KZN DARD prescriptive split [T1 KZN DARD Tomato §Nitrogen]:
- N: ¼ at planting; bulk in first 6–8 wk at 2–3 wk intervals; lighter dressings next 6 wk. Target N ≥250 kg/ha at 50 t/ha; +5 kg N per +2 t yield.
- P: 40–60 kg P/ha if soil P built up; band on acid soils.
- K: ≥100 kg K/ha; +10 kg K per +2 t over 40 t/ha. Avoid Cl forms (quality).

### Adjustments

| Factor | Adjustment | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Cycle length (det 18 wk → indet 25 wk) | Total N rises 220 → 284 kg/ha (+29 %); K rises 360 → 458 kg/ha (+27 %) | [T1 Starke Ayres 2019 Tables 4–6] |

| Excess N | delays maturity, favours disease, reduces fruit size | [T1 KZN DARD Tomato §N — "Excess nitrogen…"] |
| High-Cl water | Reduces fruit quality; switch to Cl-free K (KNO3, K2SO4) | [T1 KZN DARD Tomato §K] |
| Drought / poor Ca translocation | BER; Ca foliar at fruit set | [T1 KZN DARD Tomato §Ca] |

### Notes
- **Blossom-end rot (BER)**: Ca translocation problem — high-K soils, drought, swing in soil moisture. Ca foliar sprays + steady soil moisture; high-Ca cv selection. [T1 KZN DARD §Ca; T2 Yara Tomato]
- **Magnesium / potassium antagonism**: Excess K → induced Mg deficiency on lower leaves. [T1 KZN DARD §K and §Mg]
- **Tunnel-vs-field**: tunnel populations 2× open-field, drives proportional N/K demand → use indeterminate program (Table 6) as starting point.
- **Strong determinate** types react aggressively to early N; peak earlier than indeterminate (Table 4 comments).

| Factor | Adjustment | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Heavy rain / sandy soils | N split into 4–5 dressings to avoid leach | [T1 Kynoch SA — "heavy rainfall leaches nitrates"] |
| Seed crop vs ware | +30 % P (set more tubers, smaller size) | [T2 Haifa] |

### Notes
- Single most important SA management practice: leaf/petiole analysis at emergence and at bulking start, with N top-dress decisions made on the lab result (not pre-planned) [T1 RegenZ SA Potato; T1 Kynoch].
- Scab on tubers strongly tied to pH > 6.8 + dry conditions during tuber initiation.
- Cultivar matters massively: Mondial / Sifra (long season, high N response) vs BP1 / Up-to-Date (shorter, lower input). No public per-cv N curve in SA.
- **CIPC (chlorpropham) sprout-suppressant timing**: applied post-harvest in store, not in-season; out of scope for nutrient programme but flag for client deliverable.
